Chapter 6 - Exercises

Chapter 6 - Exercises These are my solutions to the exercises from chapter 6. Easy. 6E1. State the three motivating criteria that define information entropy. Information entropy (a measure of uncertainty) should be continous. A small change in probability should also lead to only a small change in uncertainty. We don’t want to allow for sudden jumps. increasing as the number of possible events increases. That means, if only one event has a very high chance of happening and all other have only a very small chance, then there is little uncertainty in what comes next and thus more less information. »
